Lab Sciences
Course: Elementary Science
Grade Level: 3-4 (advanced 2nd graders are welcome to register) 
Day/Time: Monday 9:00 – 10:15am OR 12:00 - 1:15pm
Instructor: Kim Crossman
Cost: $42 per month + $35 registration fee
Book: Land Animals of the 6th Day (Zoology 3, Exploring Creation), by Jeanne Fulbright, ISBN# 978-1-932012-85-9 and Land Animals Notebooking Journal, by Jeanne Fulbright, ISBN# 978-1-935495-13-0. Order from Apologia at www.highschoolscience.com or call 1-888-524-4724.  You can also go to www.christianbooks.com for lower prices or order used online. 
Description: Does your child love experiments and science labs? This course will excite any young child by introducing them to God's creation using an exciting new series of books, by Jeannie Fulbright, through Apologia. We are following a four year rotation of books in this series. This year's book is Land Animals of the 6th Day. Other books to be used in the following years include: Astronomy, Flying Creatures of the 5th Day (we used in the 2009-2010 year) and Exploring Creation with Botany (we used in the 2010-2011 year). We will also supplement each year with a unit study on systems of the human body. We will include new material on the human body each of the four years in the rotation. Students will participate in a hands-on lab every week, along with a Science Fair at the end of the year. In order to begin developing accountability, a small amount of homework will be assigned each week. Also, in order to give the young student a chance to develop their test taking skills, tests are given every four to five weeks in a very relaxed classroom environment.

Course: Elementary Choir and Music Appreciation
Grade Level: 3-6 (advanced 6th graders are encouraged to take our middle school ensemble)
Day/Time:  Monday 2:50 – 4:05pm
Instructor: Joy Gibbons
Cost: $20 per month + $20 registration fee
Book: A $10 fee for music will be charged to your first month’s bill (this will cover the entire year).
Description: This class is foundational for a lifetime of loving music. We will explore the concepts of rhythm, pitch, note-reading, and music appreciation in a fun and friendly environment. Students will enjoy playing instruments, singing songs from all over the world and learning to work together as a choir. Large motor skills, ear-training, correct vocal placement, mathematical concepts, reading skills and mental retention are all exercised and improved upon as the year progresses. Students and their families are offered field trip opportunities throughout the year and will participate in performances throughout the community. What a fun way to learn! "Early musical training helps develop brain areas involved in language and reasoning. It is thought that brain development continues for many years after birth. Recent studies have clearly indicated that musical training physically develops the part of the left side of the brain known to be involved with processing language.” (Carolyn Phillips, Twelve Benefits of Music Education.)
